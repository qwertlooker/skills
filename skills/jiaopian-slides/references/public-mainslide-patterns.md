# Public business mainslide calibration

Use this reference to calibrate Huawei-style work against the official public 2025 Huawei ICT Talent Ecosystem Mainslides light version. Treat the observations as transferable patterns, not as a template or universal corporate mandate.

## Patterns worth transferring

- Default to a white or high-key canvas for ordinary customer and stakeholder meetings.
- Use the complete takeaway title in Huawei red on routine business mainslides. Do not use a top strip or title underline as a substitute for hierarchy.
- Combine one large proof object with compact supporting evidence: image plus figures, trend plus metrics, process plus platform, matrix plus learning path, or case timeline plus results.
- Count red emphasis by semantic group. A full red title may coexist with one red KPI family, one selected path, or one result block.
- Use gray as structural scaffolding: dividers, table grids, inactive stages, perspective bases, and secondary labels.
- Prefer real evidence imagery on content pages: customer photos, product images, event photos, platform screenshots, publications, or sourced diagrams.
- Use section dividers sparingly: one large gray number, one short section name, and one relevant image or illustration.
- Keep the footer compact and subordinate. Copy neither Huawei marks nor security labels without authorization.

## Do not copy blindly

- Do not copy the Huawei logo, proprietary/confidential footer, security level, QR codes, partner logos, customer names, quotes, results, or internal identifiers.
- Do not reproduce page-specific 3D arrows, gradients, dotted red boxes, or ornamental ellipses merely because they appear in a public deck. Keep them only when matching an approved reference or when the geometry explains a mechanism.
- Do not force a short CIO briefing to match a 62-page information library. Borrow the title hierarchy, evidence discipline, and page silhouettes; retain leadership pacing and lower density.
- Do not use generated imagery as customer or product evidence. Generated visuals are acceptable only for clearly conceptual covers or dividers.

## Calibration for a short leadership deck

1. Use a light/high-key cover by default; permit a dark cover only for an explicit stage or dark-visual route.
2. Put the conclusion and requested decision early.
3. Give every content page one decisive proof object. If the input lacks data, show `示意`, `待客户确认`, or `建议口径` visibly.
4. Use full red takeaway titles unless the deck is explicitly classified as neutral technical.
5. Prefer aligned bands, evidence matrices, architecture layers, or timelines over repeated rounded cards and oversized arrows.
6. Close on the decision, next action, or measurable gate rather than a generic “谢谢”.

## Review comparison

When comparing a generated deck with the public mainslides, classify gaps under:

- **Message:** conclusion strength, proof, implication, and decision clarity.
- **Title system:** full red business title vs explicitly selected neutral technical title.
- **Evidence:** real source, metric family, case proof, dated baseline, or clearly labeled placeholder.
- **Composition:** one dominant proof object, aligned structure, purposeful whitespace, and varied silhouettes.
- **Red semantics:** title plus one related content group, not scattered decoration.
- **Brand safety:** logo, footer, QR code, customer material, and confidentiality handling.
- **Production:** editable objects, font slots, overflow, source notes, and full-size render QA.
