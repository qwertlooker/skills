# Source register and confidence

## Practical inferences from public decks

These are design observations, not quoted corporate mandates:

- conclusion-led or claim-like page titles outperform topic labels;
- diagrams and categorized evidence dominate routine content pages;
- light canvas, dark text, gray structure, and selective red accents form the most reusable default;
- architecture pages may be dense, but their hierarchy remains explicit;
- cover/divider pages can carry stronger imagery while routine content pages stay flatter and more structured.
- a full red title can coexist with one semantically related family of red figures or labels; count emphasis by meaning, not by the number of red objects;
- short leadership decks should borrow the hierarchy and evidence discipline, not the 62-page information density.

## Unverified claims to avoid presenting as universal rules

- "Every Jiaopian deck must use exactly one layout grid."
- "All slide titles must be 32 pt in every business unit."
- "Jiaopian red is always `RGB 153/0/0`."
- "Every page must include the Jiaopian logo or a proprietary footer."
- "Jiaopian universally mandates a specific number of bullets, cards, icons, or pages."

If an approved business-unit template conflicts with a practical default, follow the template.
